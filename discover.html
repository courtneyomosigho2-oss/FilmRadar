The Journey of Motion Pictures

The history of cinema is a fascinating timeline of technological breakthroughs and artistic shifts. It began in the late 19th century with the Kinetoscope and the Cinématographe, moving from short, silent clips to the "Talkies" during the late 1920s. This transition revolutionised how stories were told, shifting away from pure visual pantomime to dialogue-driven narratives.

As we moved into the mid-20th century, the introduction of Technicolor and widescreen formats like CinemaScope were direct responses to the rising popularity of television. Filmmakers realised that to keep audiences in theaters, they needed to provide an experience that was "larger than life." Today, in 2026, we see a similar shift as Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) begin to influence how directors frame their shots.

Cinema Terms Every Fan Should Know

Mise-en-scène

A French term meaning "placing on stage." It refers to the things that appear before the camera, like set design, lighting, and costume.

Cinematography

The art of photography and camerawork in filmmaking, such as the choice of film stock, lenses and camera angles.

The "MacGuffin"

An object or device in a movie that serves as a trigger for the plot, popularised by Alfred Hitchcock.

Post-Production

The phase after filming where editing, sound mixing, and visual effects (VFX) are added to the final cut.
